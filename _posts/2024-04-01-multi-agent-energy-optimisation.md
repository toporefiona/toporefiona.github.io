My MEng thesis developed a MATLAB-based multi-agent system to model 
and reduce industrial energy consumption. The core challenge was 
getting multiple agents to negotiate load-shifting in a way that 
reduced total consumption without destabilising the system. The 
project applied control systems theory and data analysis to improve 
simulated efficiency while maintaining operational targets. This work 
consolidated a genuine interest in computational approaches to building 
performance and energy systems, and directly informs my current 
direction into data-driven energy analytics.
